The Dream — i.

A belief passed down like a seed.

This mission was first inspired by my great-grandfather's belief that service to others is the greatest purpose of life. A conviction he lived without fanfare, teaching by doing — that kindness compounds, and that the smallest act, done consistently, can shape a community.

That belief was handed down, generation to generation, like a seed wrapped in cloth. Today it has grown roots, and leaves, and a name: RKJ Foundation.

"Service to others is the greatest purpose of life." — A belief, three generations strong
Dr. D.N. Sharma
Founder & Patron
The Journey — ii.

Today, my grandfather continues to carry that dream forward.

Even in his later years, he remains devoted to helping people and creating small but meaningful change in the community. He rarely speaks of it. He simply shows up — for the elderly neighbour, the young student, the worker waiting quietly at the door.

The foundation is the shape his devotion takes. Everything we do is an attempt to match the steadiness of his hands with the scale of what's possible.

With love, his family — and all who walk alongside.
What We Do — iii.

Different people. Different needs. One long walk.

Through this journey we try to support people in different ways. We don't specialise in a single cause — life rarely does. Wherever a hand is needed, we try to be there.

abandoned
Meals, shelter, a place to sit without being turned away. We work alongside elders and those who have slipped through the cracks.

Safe spaces, small grants, and patient mentorship. The long game — helping a family find its own footing rather than depend on ours.
03
Dignity for
workers
Advocating for fair treatment, rest days, and medical access for daily-wage and informal workers in the communities we serve.
04

A quiet act of faith — returning something to the land we borrow. Native species, community-led plantings, monsoon drives.
05

camps
Free check-ups, vision screenings, and basic medical access — organised where hospitals are farther than they should be.
06

& mentorship
Guiding young minds. Tutoring, scholarships, career conversations — and a gentle push when they need it most.
